“Growing On Me” - 3D Animation Short Film

Team Project | Simon Fraser University | Jan 2021 - Apr 2021

Description

As a team of four, we had four months to learn everything about 3D animation from scratch, including what 3D means, how to use Maya, and how to animate and film characters cinematically.

What and how I contributed to the team

Story Co-Planning

We first worked on Google Doc to plan out the plot and prepare a shotlist (Story Co-lead).

After receiving sketches from an artist member, I modeled it in Maya. Below is the preliminary version of the model before undergoing multiple feedback sessions with the team.

Character Modelling

When the character was ready, I moved on to the next steps:

Rigging, Weight Painting and Texturing

Animating

A side project I made to learn animation in Maya. This is arguably the most fundamental technique every 3D animation artist must know.

Another side project to learn to animate a jump, with high-fidelity rendering.

Overall, I was in charge of animating 30% of the whole short movie, including this watering part using nParticles.

Set Modelling

I also designed and modeled the environmental set with three species of tree, a pond, and an XGen grass. These were later multiplied and reused in different scenes of the movie.

Challenges

Working on this intense project remotely imposed a huge challenge to four of us. Since it was during the COVID19 pandemic, everyone was in different regions with different time zones so we all had to make compromises. Issues emerged as soon as we tried to set up a shared cloud-based folder that housed hundreds of small files, such as missing filles during transfers, delayed synchronizations, and folder- and file-related conflicts. However, thanks to patience, effective, and responsible communication, we managed to help each other out and get over the issues quickly.

Those were on top of the fact that everything 3D was brand new to us. Due to little experience, everyone had to do tons of research and scheduled numerous Discord meetings to help give feedback and fix issues. Since each individual was accountable and independent, the workflow was smooth, tasks were delivered on time, and no meeting run without a member missing.

What I’ve learnt

Starting with absolute zero experience in 3D and Maya was a big hurdle. To catch up, I constantly challenged myself by rushing through various research tasks, engaging in self-learning, drawing lessons and challenges from LinkedIn Learning and YouTube, and testing my skills under time pressure. These experiences honed my ability to perform quick information look-ups, sift through irrelevant content, and manage stress levels by maintaining a balance between work and rest. Ultimately, I believe that mental (and physical) health and life skills are just as vital as technical expertise.

At the end, our invested time and effort paid off when the animation earned us a score of 29/30. I ended the term with an A, and I’m confident my teammates achieved similar results. Overall, everything turned out as expected, and I’m happy to walk away from the course as a much-improved version of myself.
