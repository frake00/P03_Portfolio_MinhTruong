“Growing On Me” - 3D Animation Short Film

Individual project | Simon Fraser University | May 2020 – Aug 2020

Description

This first time I put my hands on a motion-graphic project was challenging, not only since it was new, but also because I had to complete it under an intersession course’s pressure. The goal was to build a kinetic typography, in under 3 weeks (alongside with other courses, of courses), from any source of audio. It could be any audio, from a clip or a movie scene cut, a song,… The project was designed to give students a chance to turn learnt theories in layout, sequence, color, 2D effect, transition, and the animation principles into real project.

Design Process

1. First I edited the sound using Audacity, cut and merged some parts, ensuring it’s smoothly compressed from 6.26 minutes to 65 seconds.

2. I then used Word to build a word-by-word transcript for more conveniently transitioning them to the motion video later.

3. The first part of the video is where I tested out my first design technique – build the graphics in Adobe Illustrator then import them to After Effect. The technique was a result of my hours researching on YouTube and was believed to better the design.

4. Later, it turned out that, despite the advanced control I have over the design, the image quality is wrecked after every importation. I then had to move to the next approach – work directly on AE, with, of course, less fancy stuff. That’s why you can spot the distinct difference before and after the second 0.20 in the YouTube video.

Motion graphic breakdowns

To conclude what I’ve learnt

The project added invaluable practice to my pathway of becoming a better self-learner. I got chances to perform a great deal of research looking for best approaches, try some and hash the odd ones out. From that, I learnt how to deal with counter-productivity, drawbacks, and technical issues along the way, and how to solve them in a timely manner. Every hands-on experience not only naturally enhances my technical skills but I myself can always be aware of what I can draw from to improve my soft set. By practicing those more often, I grow my inner art enthusiast more swiftly and solidly.
